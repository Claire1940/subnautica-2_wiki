## The Publisher Drama: Krafton and Unknown Worlds

To understand why the May launch is happening now, we have to look behind the scenes at the relationship between the developer, Unknown Worlds, and their publisher, Krafton. Recent reports indicated a period of significant tension, including the temporary removal of CEO Ted Gill and other senior leadership by Krafton in 2025. While a Delaware court eventually ordered their reinstatement, the friction has left some fans worried about the state of the game.

Crucially for players, there is a $69.8 million revenue threshold in the contract between the two companies. If the studio hits this revenue target, the development team is entitled to a substantial bonus payout. This financial incentive suggests that while the publisher may have wanted to rush the launch to hit quarterly goals, the developers are incentivized to ensure the game is high-quality enough to maintain long-term sales.

Despite the corporate "chaos," the game is no longer listed as being published by Krafton on certain platforms like Steam, suggesting a return to more independent operations for Unknown Worlds. This is great news for fans who were worried that the **subnautica 2 early access price** might include hidden "live service" elements pushed by a large publisher.
