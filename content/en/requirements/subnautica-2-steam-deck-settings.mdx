The core of finding the best **subnautica 2 steam deck settings** lies in the Graphics menu. Unlike its predecessor, Subnautica 2 utilizes more advanced volumetric effects and higher-fidelity textures, which can quickly overwhelm the handheld if left at "High" or "Ultra" presets.

| Setting | Recommended Value | Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Window Mode** | Windowed Fullscreen | High stability |
| **Resolution** | 1280x800 | Native / Best Clarity |
| **FPS Limit** | 60 (In-game) / 30-45 (SteamOS) | Frame Pacing |
| **Anti-Aliasing** | TAA (Performance) | Smoother edges |
| **Upscaling** | Off or FSR 2.1 (Balanced) | Performance boost |
| **Texture Quality** | Medium | Visual balance |
| **Shadow Quality** | Low | High FPS gain |
| **Volumetric Fog** | Low | Significant GPU relief |

To achieve a consistent 45 FPS on the OLED model or 30 FPS on the LCD model, we recommend starting with a **Low Preset** and then manually bumping **Texture Quality** to Medium. This keeps the world looking sharp while the lower shadow and fog settings provide the necessary overhead for complex underwater scenes.

<Callout type="warning" title="Note on Upscaling">
While FSR (FidelityFX Super Resolution) can increase frame rates, it may introduce "shimmering" on the water's surface in Subnautica 2. If the image looks too grainy, stick to **800p Native with TAA**.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Optimization Guide">

Follow these steps to ensure your Steam Deck is fully optimized for Subnautica 2. This process covers both internal game settings and external SteamOS tweaks.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Update System Software">
    Navigate to Settings > System and ensure you are on the latest Stable or Preview channel. Steam OS 3.9+ includes specific driver optimizations for newer titles like Subnautica 2.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Set Compatibility Layer">
    Go to the game's properties in your library, select **Compatibility**, and check "Force the use of a specific Steam Play compatibility tool." Select **Proton 11.0 Beta**.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Configure SteamOS Performance Overlay">
    Press the Quick Access Button (three dots), go to the Battery icon, and set the **Frame Limit** to 45. This matches the OLED's 90Hz refresh rate perfectly (2:1) and provides a much smoother feel than 30 FPS.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="In-Game Graphical Adjustment">
    Launch the game and navigate to Settings > Graphics. Disable all "Experimental" features and set the preset to Low. Manually increase **Texture Detail** to Medium if you have the 4GB UMA Buffer enabled.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Performance Gain">
By limiting the refresh rate to 45Hz/45FPS, you can extend your battery life by up to 25% compared to running uncapped, while maintaining a much smoother experience than a 30FPS lock.
</Callout>

</Section>
