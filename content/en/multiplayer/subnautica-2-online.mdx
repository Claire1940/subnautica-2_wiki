<Section icon={Swords} title="Cooperative Gameplay Mechanics and Shared Resources">

In **Subnautica 2 online**, cooperation is more than just swimming together; it is about managing a shared ecosystem. The game is designed to be resource-rich to accommodate multiple players, ensuring that the scarcity of materials doesn't hinder progression. However, certain high-tech systems are shared across the entire lobby, requiring communication between Pioneers.

One critical edge case is the **Scanner Station**. While highly effective for locating specific resources like Lead or Silver, the station's output is universal. If one player sets the scanner to look for Quartz, every player in the session will see those Quartz nodes on their HUD. Only one resource can be tracked at a time per station, making it a shared tool rather than an individual one.

<CardGrid cols={3}>
  <Card title="Shared Respawn Points">
    Multiple players can assign themselves to the same **Bio Pod** or base bed. Upon death, you will reprint at your last assigned location.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Resource Distribution">
    The world of Subnautica 2 "showers" players with resources. Scarcity is rarely an issue even with a full 4-player team.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Independent Exploration">
    With no invisible tethers, one player can be at the surface farming food while another is 600m deep exploring ruins.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

<Callout type="warning" title="Resource Management">
While resources are plentiful, the **Scanner Station** limitation means you should coordinate with your team. If everyone is looking for different materials simultaneously, you will need to build multiple bases with their own scanner stations to avoid HUD clutter.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Progression: Genetic Adaptations and Tools">

Progression in Subnautica 2 has evolved beyond simple oxygen tank upgrades. The core of your survival now lies in **Genetic Adaptations**. By interacting with the **Adaptation Pavilion** and various "Angel Trees" found in deep coves, players can modify their DNA to withstand the harsh alien environment.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Obtain the Multi-Tool & Scanner">
    Collect Fibrous Pulp and Acid Pouches to craft basic batteries. The Scanner is your most important tool for unlocking blueprints and studying the local fauna.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Locate the Welcome Center">
    Find the Alterra Welcome Center to unlock the **Habitat Builder** and the **Bio Lab**. Powering this building is essential for early-game tech.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Acquire First Adaptations">
    Visit the Adaptation room in the initial wreck. The first major upgrade allows you to breathe highly pressurized air, essential for diving past 500m.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Unlock Bio Mods">
    Use the **Bio Scanner** on creatures like the Hammerhead or the Sea Slug. This unlocks passive abilities like "Glow in the Dark" or "Increased Swim Speed."
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Adaptation Type | Effect | Requirement |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Pressure Resistance** | Dive deeper without damage | Initial Wreck Pavilion |
| **Digestive System** | Eat alien species safely | Jelly Plant Root |
| **Heat Resistance** | Survive Lava Biomes | Cured World Tree Root |
| **Advanced Vision** | Read Alien (Axiom) text | Second Infected Tree |
| **Oxygen Boost** | Slower O2 consumption | Bio Lab Research |

<Callout type="success" title="Survival Essential">
Always carry a **Sonic Resonator**. This tool is required to mine larger ore veins and is the only way to cure the infected tree roots that block your progression into the deeper narrative areas.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Base Building and the Tadpole Submersible">

Base building in **Subnautica 2 online** is a collaborative effort. The new Habitat Builder allows for more complex structures, including vertical corridors and specialized rooms like the **Moonpool** for your vehicles. The primary vehicle for early to mid-game exploration is the **Tadpole**, a versatile submersible that can be upgraded with various chassis modules.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="The Tadpole Submersible">
    - **Capacity**: Supports up to 3 players (1 pilot, 2 on exterior arms).
    - **Customization**: Can be fitted with Scout Ray or Winged chassis.
    - **Speed**: High mobility but requires frequent power cell swaps.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Habitat Infrastructure">
    - **Power**: Solar, Bio-reactors, and Hydroelectric turbines.
    - **Utility**: Scanner stations, modification stations, and battery chargers.
    - **Social**: Round table rooms and decorative posters.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

Managing power is the biggest challenge for a multi-player base. With four players using fabricators and chargers, a single solar panel will not suffice. You must transition to **Hydroelectric Turbines** placed in underwater currents or **Thermal Generators** in volcanic vents to maintain a steady energy supply.

| Vehicle Module | Function | Resource Cost |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Depth Module MK1** | Increases depth limit | Titanium, Silver, Glass |
| **Scout Chassis** | Massive speed increase | Lithium, Sulfur |
| **Storage Module** | Adds external cargo space | Titanium Ingot |
| **Winged Chassis** | Improved maneuverability | Lead, Quartz |

<Callout type="info" title="Power Tip">
Use **Power Transmitters** to link distant energy sources (like thermal vents) to your main base. This allows you to build in safe, shallow areas while drawing power from dangerous, high-energy biomes.
</Callout>
