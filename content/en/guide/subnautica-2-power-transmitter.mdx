<Section icon={Zap} title="Understanding Power Transmitters in Subnautica 2">

Power Transmitters in Subnautica 2 are essential conduits designed to extend the reach of your power generation. Unlike the more limited range of their predecessors in the original Subnautica and Below Zero, these new transmitters offer remarkable flexibility and extended connection distances. They act as a bridge, carrying power from a generator located in a resource-rich area, such as a strong current for hydroelectric turbines or a hydrothermal vent, directly to your distant base. This system significantly simplifies base planning and allows for greater freedom in choosing base locations, even if they are far from immediate power sources.

<Callout type="tip" title="Enhanced Range">

<Section icon={Target} title="How to Place and Connect Your Power Transmitter">

Placing and connecting a **Subnautica 2 Power Transmitter** is a straightforward process, but it requires attention to detail to ensure proper power flow. The primary goal is to establish a clear conduit from your power generator to your base. This involves first identifying your power source, then strategically placing transmitters to bridge the gap.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Identify Your Power Source">
    First, locate a suitable power generation site. This could be an area with strong currents for a **hydroelectric turbine** or a hydrothermal vent for a thermal plant. Ensure your chosen generator is placed and active.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Place the First Transmitter Near the Generator">
    Deploy your first power transmitter close to your active power generator. The transmitter will automatically connect to the generator if it's within range, indicated by a visible power line.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Chain Transmitters Towards Your Base">
    If your base is far, you'll need to chain multiple transmitters. Place subsequent transmitters within range of the previous one, gradually extending the power line towards your base. The length of these connections is quite generous.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Connect to Your Base">
    The final transmitter in your chain must be placed directly onto your base module. Look for a visual indicator or a change in the transmitter's appearance when it properly snaps onto the base. This direct connection is crucial for power transfer. If you see a small pole at the bottom of the transmitter when placed near the base, it's not connected. It needs to sit directly on top.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Connection Check">
If the power transmitter isn't sitting directly on your base module, it won't transfer power. Make sure it visually connects, often by snapping into place, rather than just being nearby.
</Callout>

</Section>

### Troubleshooting Common Power Issues

| Issue                        | Possible Cause                                      | Solution                                                                |
| :--------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Base not receiving power** | Transmitter not connected directly to base.         | Reposition the final transmitter to snap onto a base module.            |
| **Intermittent power loss**  | Gap in transmitter chain, or generator offline.     | Check all transmitter connections; ensure generator is active and fueled. |
| **Low power output**         | Generator not producing enough power for demand.    | Add more generators or optimize existing ones (e.g., better current).   |
| **Transmitter appears buggy**| Glitch in placement or connection.                  | Pick up and re-place the problematic transmitter and its neighbors.     |
