<Section icon={Map} title="The Thermal Zone: A Volcanic Wonderland">
The Thermal Zone is a dynamic and often dangerous region characterized by its intense geothermal activity. This expansive area is divided into several sub-biomes, each with its own distinct features and dangers, making it a critical early-game area for resources but also a significant threat.

The Thermal Zone stretches across a significant portion of the map, encompassing thermal spires, purple vents, and tree spires. Hydrothermal vents spew hot gases, creating a unique visual and environmental challenge. The purple vents, in particular, display striking yellow discolorations due to hydrothermal alteration and vibrant purple hues from bacteria and rare minerals, hinting at nascent life forms.

<Callout type="warning" title="Thermal Hazards">

<Section icon={Shield} title="Delta Island & Copa Mining Site: Secrets of Alterra">
Delta Island stands as a rugged landmass, contrasting sharply with the aquatic biomes, and holds significant narrative importance. Beneath its surface lies the Copa Mining Site, a relic of Alterra's clandestine operations.

Delta Island is characterized by its snow-covered terrain, biting winds, and damp rock caverns. While harsh, it offers thermal lilies and hot springs to ward off the cold. This island is home to the Delta Research Station, a pivotal location for understanding Alterra's presence on Planet 4546B and their secretive motives regarding Precursor technology and the Kharaa bacterium.

<Callout type="tip" title="Stay Warm on Delta Island">
Utilize **Thermal Lilies** and hot springs to maintain body temperature while exploring Delta Island's frigid surface. A cold suit is highly recommended for extended expeditions.
</Callout>

Beneath Delta Island, the Copa Mining Site reveals a sprawling underground facility. This area is rich in lore, offering glimpses into Alterra's past mining and research activities. Players can find abandoned equipment, computer terminals, and rail systems, piecing together the story of their predecessors.

<Section icon={Swords} title="The Arctic: Icy Depths and Towering Spires">
The Arctic biome, encompassing both underwater regions and the towering Arctic Spires above ground, presents a stark, beautiful, and dangerous environment. It's a testament to the planet's diverse extremes.

The underwater Arctic is defined by rugged, rocky seafloors and massive icebergs. Depths range from 0 to 320 meters, teeming with unique aquatic life adapted to the cold, including gargantuan Titan Holefish and aggressive Chelicerates. The sheer beauty of the icy underwater calderas and the vastness of the cold waters are truly breathtaking.

<Callout type="warning" title="Ice Worm Threat">
When traversing the Arctic Spires, be extremely cautious of **Ice Worms**. These enormous Leviathan-class predators burrow through the ice and ambush prey. Move slowly and quietly to avoid detection, as they hunt by sound.
</Callout>

Northwest of the underwater Arctic lie the Arctic Spires, a labyrinthine landmass of flat ice sheets broken by colossal ice spires. This surface region is home to the terrifying Ice Worms, making surface travel a high-stakes endeavor. Despite the danger, the views from these spires are unparalleled, offering a unique perspective of Planet 4546B.

<Section icon={Target} title="Twisty Bridges: Bioluminescent Wonders">
The Twisty Bridges biome is an early-game marvel, known for its stunning bioluminescence and unique geological formations. This area offers both aesthetic beauty and crucial resources for early progression.

This biome is characterized by massive, looping bridge-like structures covered in glowing algae. The entire zone has a vibrant blue tint, especially at night, creating an unforgettable underwater spectacle. It's home to ribbon plants and oxygen plants, which are vital for sustained exploration at deeper levels without advanced vehicles.

<Callout type="tip" title="Oxygen Plant Advantage">
Utilize **Oxygen Plants** in the Twisty Bridges to replenish your oxygen supply, allowing for longer dives and exploration into deeper sections without immediate need for a vehicle. This is especially useful for reaching hidden areas like Sanctuary Zero.
</Callout>

The Twisty Bridges also house Sanctuary Zero, a hidden Architect facility. This location plays a significant role in the game's narrative, as it's where players receive an SOS signal and begin their quest to find an AI Cube. The biome's varied depths, from shallow to deep purple-hued zones, ensure there's always something new to discover.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Crystal Caves & Fabricator Caverns: Deep Wonders">
For those brave enough to venture into the deepest and most alien parts of Planet 4546B, the Crystal Caves and Fabricator Caverns await. These biomes are visually stunning but incredibly challenging to navigate.

These two interconnected biomes are immediately recognizable by their massive purple and red crystal columns. The Crystal Caves are accessed via the Deep Purple Vents or Lilypad Crevice, leading to depths exceeding 500 meters. The Fabricator Caverns are even more secluded, found through small openings within the Crystal Caves themselves. Both are guarded by formidable Shadow Leviathans.

<Callout type="info" title="Navigating Crystal Biomes">
The dense crystal formations can make navigation difficult. Consider marking paths with beacons or deploying scanner room cameras to map out the intricate cave systems in the **Crystal Caves** and **Fabricator Caverns**.
</Callout>

Despite their depth and the dangers, these biomes are surprisingly rich in life, featuring bladderfish, triops, and unique plant life like ribbon plants and eclipse roots. The Crystal Caves also contain the Crystal Caves Cache, a crucial Architect base for story progression, holding vital components for the Architect ship.

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Glacial Basin: A Diverse Icy Tundra">
The Glacial Basin is arguably one of Subnautica 2's most impressive and diverse biomes, offering a blend of surface exploration and deep underwater mysteries.

This expansive surface zone features rocky exteriors, tall icy cliffs, and frozen waterfalls. It's a vast area perfect for using a Snowfox bike, with hidden valleys, blue ice caves, and glacial forests. Below the surface, the Glacial Connection extends over 200 meters deep, revealing stunning Arctic kelp forests and eye jellies that create a breathtaking bioluminescent display.

<Callout type="success" title="Rich Lore Opportunity">
The **FI Excavation Site** within the Glacial Basin is a goldmine for lore. Explore this facility to uncover secrets about the gargantuan super Leviathan frozen in ice and the ongoing research into the Kharaa bacterium.
</Callout>

A highlight of the Glacial Basin is the FI Excavation Site, a massive research facility built into a mountain. Here, players can discover the remains of a super Leviathan infected with Kharaa, providing crucial insights into the planet's history and the efforts to combat the bacterium. The combination of surface exploration, intricate cave systems, and deep lore makes this biome truly exceptional.

</Section>

<Section icon={Map} title="Lilypad Islands: A Vibrant Green Oasis">
The Lilypad Islands biome offers a stark contrast to the icy regions, presenting a vibrant, green, and active ecosystem that extends from the surface to significant depths.

This extremely green biome is dominated by gigantic circular lily pads floating on the surface, with massive veiny trunks extending deep into the water. Sunlight streams through the gaps, illuminating a dense and rocky seafloor covered in lush plant life. This area is teeming with diverse creatures, including Squidsharks, Spinnerfish, and the notorious Sea Monkeys.

<Callout type="info" title="Sea Monkey Encounters">
Be aware that **Sea Monkeys** in the Lilypad Islands are known to try and steal tools from players. Keep your tools equipped or stored away when in their vicinity to avoid losing them.
</Callout>

Beneath the main lily pad structures, decaying lily pads and bustling ecosystems lead to the Lilypad Caves. These caves, often with an ominous green glow, suggest the presence of Kharaa. Deeper still, the Deep Lilypad Caves, at 600 meters, feature unique flora like grand cotton anemones, creating a distinct and beautiful bioluminescent environment. This biome is a popular spot for base building due to its serene yet active atmosphere.

</Section>
