## Essential Preparation for Deep-Sea Scavenging

Before you head out to hunt for fragments, preparation is the difference between life and death. Based on player experiences, you should always carry a Seaglider to increase your mobility when navigating around hostile fauna. 

*   **Oxygen Management:** Ensure your tank is upgraded or carry an extra air bladder.
*   **Safety Gear:** Bring a Rebreather to mitigate oxygen consumption at lower depths.
*   **Tool Check:** Never leave your base without a fully charged battery in your scanner.
*   **Wildlife Awareness:** Keep an eye out for aggressive creatures like Brute Sharks; they are often found lurking near the wreckage where fragments are hidden.

## Navigating the Environment Safely

When searching for the **subnautica 2 habitat builder**, you will often find yourself heading into deeper, darker waters. It is common for fragments to be located in abandoned supply capsules or scattered among wreckage at depths of 100 to 150 meters. 

Players have noted that relying on visual cues, such as glowing lights or metallic glints in the seabed, can help you spot items from a distance. If you are feeling overwhelmed, stick to the shallower shelves first. As you gain more confidence and better equipment, you can venture into the more treacherous zones where resources are more abundant. ## Advanced Base Building Strategies

Once you have secured your tool, the real fun begins. Efficient base building is about more than just aesthetics; it is about functionality. Focus on creating a central hub that provides power (via solar panels or bioreactors) and sufficient storage capacity. 

Many veterans recommend building near thermal vents to ensure a constant power supply. Remember that your base must have structural integrity. As you add more rooms and windows, your base becomes more fragile. Reinforce your walls with foundations or bulkhead doors to prevent flooding and keep your sanctuary safe from the immense water pressure.

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

Even the best explorers face challenges. If you find yourself unable to place a structure, check your structural integrity meter. If it hits zero, your base will begin to leak. 

*   **Missing Ingredients:** Always keep a supply of Titanium and Copper on hand; you will need them constantly for construction.
*   **Power Outages:** If your equipment stops working, ensure your solar panels are in an area with adequate sunlight or that your bioreactor is fueled.
*   **Fragment Not Spawning:** If you cannot find a fragment in a documented location, try exiting and re-entering the area, as sometimes procedural generation can delay object loading.

**Q: Do I need multiple fragments to unlock the subnautica 2 habitat builder?**
A: No, in most current versions, you only need to scan a single fragment to unlock the full blueprint for the builder tool.

**Q: Where is the easiest place to find the builder tool early on?**

**Q: What should I do if I encounter aggressive sharks while searching?**

**Q: Can I build my base anywhere in the game?**
