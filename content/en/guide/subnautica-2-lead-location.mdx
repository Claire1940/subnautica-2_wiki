<Section icon={Target} title="Navigating to the Lead Ravine">
Reaching the primary lead location is straightforward, even for players early in their Subnautica 2 journey. Follow these steps to ensure a safe and efficient trip.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Set Your Bearing">
    From your Lifepod, orient yourself and head **northeast**. Aim for a bearing of approximately **70-75°** on your compass. Maintain this direction.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Follow the Current">
    Continue swimming for about 150-200 meters until you notice a distinct **blue natural current** flowing below you. This current will guide you into the ravine. Swim into it.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Spot Landmarks">
    As you descend into the ravine, keep an eye out for a **colony platform construction** on your left. You might also spot a Tadpole fragment nearby. These are good indicators you're in the right area.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Collect Lead">
    Once inside the ravine, you'll find numerous **lead deposits**. Smaller chunks can be collected by hand, while larger blocks require the Sonic Resonator. If you swim further north or east along the ravine floor, you'll uncover even more lead.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Watch for Wildlife">

<Section icon={Shield} title="Crafting the Sonic Resonator with Lead">
Lead's primary early-game use is in crafting the **Sonic Resonator**. This tool is indispensable for breaking larger mineral nodes, including substantial lead deposits, and for clearing Angel Combs to gain crucial adaptations like heat resistance.

<Callout type="success" title="Why the Sonic Resonator is Essential">
The Sonic Resonator not only makes lead farming more efficient but also unlocks critical progression paths by allowing you to clear obstacles and access new areas in Subnautica 2. It's a high-priority craft.
</Callout>

**Crafting Steps for Sonic Resonator:**

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Gather Basic Materials">
    Collect basic resources like **Copper**, **Titanium**, and **Quartz**. You'll also need **Acidic Pouches** which are found in early-game biomes.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Build a Basic Habitat">
    Find Habitat blueprints (one near the Lifepod, another near the alien food adaptation) and build a basic habitat. This is necessary to construct a Processor.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Acquire Processor Blueprint">
    Head to the Old Habitat (around 320m north) to find the **Processor blueprint**. While there, also look for the **Sonic Resonator blueprints** (usually in two parts inside the habitat).
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Collect Silver for Wiring Kit">
    Near the Old Habitat, explore the adjacent cave to find **Silver**. You'll need this to craft a **Wiring Kit**, a component for the Processor and Sonic Resonator.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Build and Power the Processor">
    Craft and place your **Processor** within your habitat. Power it using **Solar Panels** (the easiest early-game power source).
  </Step>
  <Step num="6" title="Process Lead into Germanium Ingots">
    Insert **two pieces of raw lead** into the Processor to create one **Germanium Ingot**. This takes about two minutes. While Germanium Ingots can also be made with silver, lead is a direct source.
  </Step>
  <Step num="7" title="Craft the Sonic Resonator">
    With Germanium Ingots, a **Battery** (easy to get), and a **Wiring Kit** (from silver), you can now craft the **Sonic Resonator** using the Fabricator in your habitat.
  </Step>
</Steps>
