Finding **lithium** early in *Subnautica 2* can be a challenge, as it typically appears in hotter, deeper zones. **Video Highlights:**
- Requires Sonic Resonator, High-Capacity Air Tank, Basic Fins, and Wakemaker.

The Great Jaw is situated roughly **350 meters south-southwest of the Lifepod**, near a large wreckage where you can find part of the repair tool blueprint. Beyond this wreck, you'll spot a giant clam-like organism with a hollow node in its center, covered in bright purple tendons. This is where you'll find early lithium deposits.

<Callout type="warning" title="Danger Zone Ahead">
The Great Jaw is a perilous spot. Its sides will **begin to close** the moment you touch a tendon. If you don't escape in time, it can be fatal. It's highly recommended to **save your game** before attempting to harvest here.

### Navigating the Hot Caves

These areas are often patrolled by dangerous fauna like the **Marrow Breach**. These creatures are territorial and can pose a significant threat, especially if they work in groups.

### Visual Identification of Lithium

Distinguishing lithium from other resources, especially in dimly lit underwater environments, is important. While lead can appear similar, lithium has distinct characteristics:

- **Coloration**: Burnt red at the top, fading to a lighter orange at the base.
- **Shape**: Resembles a cluster of long, rectangular chunks.
- **Texture**: Often appears as large, beefy mineral deposits requiring the Sonic Resonator.

<Callout type="warning" title="Avoid Confusion">
In some dark caves, lead might look similar to lithium. Always remember that lead has distinct square protrusions, whereas lithium is characterized by its elongated, clumped rectangular forms.
</Callout>
