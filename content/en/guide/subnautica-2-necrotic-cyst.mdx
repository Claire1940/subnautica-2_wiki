<Section icon={Map} title="Identifying the Necrotic Cyst Hazard">

The **subnautica 2 necrotic cyst** is not merely a visual detail; it is a dynamic threat that reacts to the player's presence and environment. These cysts typically appear as glowing, purple-black protrusions found on organic surfaces, such as coral structures or the skin of larger fauna. In the Early Access version of the game, players first encounter these anomalies when pushing into the deeper routes where ocean currents are stronger and bioluminescence becomes the primary light source.

<Callout type="warning" title="Environmental Danger">
Cysts are highly reactive to light. Using high-intensity floodlights on your Tadpole submersible can cause them to burst, releasing spores that accelerate the infection rate in the immediate area.
</Callout>

Identifying the cyst early is the difference between a successful expedition and a forced retreat. The infection progresses through three distinct stages, each represented by different physical markers on the player's HUD and character model.

| Infection Stage | Visual Indicators | Gameplay Impact |
|:--- |:--- |:--- |
| **Stage 1: Incubation** | Small glowing spots on wrists | No immediate health loss / Minor stamina drain |
| **Stage 2: Symptomatic** | Raised purple pustules / Vein discoloration | -15% Max Health / Increased oxygen consumption |
| **Stage 3: Necrotic** | Large, pulsating cysts / Audio coughing cues | -40% Max Health / Reduced swim speed / Spore shedding |

<Callout type="tip" title="Scanner Priority">

<Section icon={Shield} title="Curing the Necrotic Cyst: Steps to Recovery">

Treating the **subnautica 2 necrotic cyst** requires a multi-step process involving resource gathering, blueprint discovery, and base expansion. You cannot simply wait for the infection to clear; it is a permanent debuff until actively neutralized.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Obtain a Biological Sample">
    Use the Survival Knife or a specialized Sample Extractor on a mature cyst found in the environment. Beware: this will release a burst of spores.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Analyze at the Research Station">
    Take the sample back to your base. You must have a Research Station (distinct from the Fabricator) to sequence the parasite's DNA.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Gather Neutralizing Agents">
    The cure requires rare materials found in the deep biomes. You will need to hunt for Coral Fungus and Deep-Sea Sulfur.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Fabricate the Bio-Neutralizer">
    Use the Medical Fabricator to create the Bio-Neutralizer dart or consumable.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Administer Treatment">
    Use the item on yourself or a teammate. For vehicles, you must use a "Hull Decontamination Kit."
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Advanced Strategy">
Once you have cured the infection once, you can craft "Cyst-Resistant Armor Plating" for your Tadpole submersible, preventing the parasite from attaching to your hull during deep-sea exploration.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Co-op Strategies for Infection Control">

With the introduction of 4-player co-op in Subnautica 2, managing the **subnautica 2 necrotic cyst** becomes a team effort. Shared inventory and shared base storage mean that one careless explorer can jeopardize the entire mission.

**Role Distribution:**
In a dedicated survival team, it is highly recommended to have one player designated as the "Medic." This player prioritizes the Medical Fabricator and maintains a stockpile of Bio-Neutralizers. Since blueprints and data entries are shared, once the Medic learns the cure, the entire group can access the knowledge, but the resource gathering remains a collective task.

**Base Isolation:**
Advanced base building allows for the construction of "Airlock Decon Chambers." These rooms automatically scan players as they enter and prevent the inner doors from opening if a Stage 2 or 3 infection is detected. This prevents the base's internal air supply from becoming contaminated.

<Callout type="tip" title="Shared Progress">
Use the "Dive Elevator" tool to transport resources from deep, infected zones back to the surface. This minimizes the number of players who need to enter high-risk areas, lowering the overall infection risk for the party.
</Callout>
