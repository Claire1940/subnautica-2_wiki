In Subnautica 2, this appliance is often referred to as the Battery Terminal, and its fragments are located in a specific abandoned facility to the North.

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Blueprint Acquisition">

Once you reach the Abandoned Laboratory, the challenge shifts from navigation to exploration. The facility is partially collapsed, and finding the specific fragments requires navigating tight corridors and multiple levels. Follow these steps to ensure you don't miss the **subnautica 2 battery charger** components.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Locate the Rear Entrance">
    Approach the back section of the large laboratory structure. Look for a small hole or breach in the hull near the seabed. This is the safest way to enter the main interior without needing advanced cutting tools.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Search the Main Laboratory Room">
    Enter the first large room. Toward the right side of the main area, you will find the first Battery Terminal fragment mounted on the wall. It looks like a small vertical dock with four slots. Use your Scanner tool to secure the first 50% of the blueprint.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Utilize Internal Air Bubbles">
    The lab is deep enough that a standard tank might run low. Locate the rising air bubbles inside the facility. Standing in these bubbles will replenish your oxygen meter, allowing you to continue your search without surfacing.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Climb to the Upper Deck">
    Locate the ladder near the first fragment. Climb to the upper level of the habitat. The second Battery Terminal fragment is usually located on the wall in this upper corridor or the adjacent small room.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Blueprint Bug Alert">
Some players in the [Steam Community](https://steamcommunity.com/app/1962700/discussions/) have reported that scanning two fragments does not always trigger the unlock. If your blueprint remains locked at 2/2, search the surrounding wrecks or the "Welcome Center" for a potential third fragment to force the unlock.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Crafting and Base Integration">

After acquiring the blueprint, you can construct the Battery Charger using the Habitat Builder tool. This appliance must be placed on an interior wall of a powered Seabase or a large vehicle like the Cyclops (if available in your current build). It is essential to ensure your base has a stable power supply, as charging four batteries simultaneously can drain small solar arrays quickly during the night.

| Material | Quantity | Purpose |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Titanium** | 2 | Structural housing for the unit |
| **Copper Wire** | 1 | Internal circuitry and power conduction |
| **Wiring Kit** | 1 | Advanced components for the charging interface |

The Battery Charger features a real-time display showing the charge percentage of each inserted battery. This allows you to swap out depleted cells from your flashlight, scanner, or Seaglide and replace them with fresh ones.

<Callout type="success" title="Power Management">
The Battery Charger is 100% efficient. This means it draws exactly 1 unit of power from your base for every 1 unit it puts into a battery. If your base power hits zero, the charger will pause and display a "No Power" message.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Advanced Usage & Optimization">

To get the most out of your **subnautica 2 battery charger**, you should understand how it interacts with different power sources and other charging appliances. While the Battery Charger handles small cells, you will eventually need the Power Cell Charger (or Energycell Loader) for your vehicles.

| Feature | Battery Charger | Power Cell Charger |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Target Items** | Standard/Ion Batteries | Standard/Ion Power Cells |
| **Slot Count** | 4 Slots | 2 Slots |
| **Base Power Draw** | Low to Moderate | High |
| **Best Location** | Near Fabricator | Near Moonpool/Dock |

Managing your power grid is vital when you start using multiple chargers. If you are relying on Solar Panels, avoid charging a full set of four batteries at night, as this can lead to a total base blackout, disabling your Oxygenators.

<Callout type="info" title="Operational Toggle">
In the latest updates, players can left-click the Battery Terminal to toggle it on or off. If your batteries aren't charging despite having base power, check the front display to ensure the status is set to "Active."
</Callout>

</Section>
