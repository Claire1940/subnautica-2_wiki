<Section icon={Map} title="Locating Natural Currents">

Finding a suitable location for your hydroelectric turbine is the most critical step. These turbines harness energy from "natural currents," which are distinct from just any open water. You'll recognize these currents by a visible blue aura surrounding them and the noticeable push they exert on your player character.

**Identifying Strong Currents:**

*   **Visual Cues:** Look for areas with a faint, shimmering blue aura in the water. These are the active current zones.
*   **Player Interaction:** When you swim into a natural current, you'll feel a distinct push or pull, indicating its direction and strength.
*   **Placement Indicator:** When attempting to place a turbine, a small number will appear, indicating the potential power output (e.g., "12"). Aim for the highest number possible.

<Callout type="warning" title="Avoid Weak Current Zones">
Placing a turbine in an area without a strong natural current will result in zero or minimal power generation. Always look for the blue aura and the power output indicator before committing to a placement.
</Callout>

It is highly recommended to scout potential base locations near these natural current sources. This strategic planning will save you significant effort and resources in the long run by simplifying your power grid.

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Setting Up Your Hydroelectric Turbine System">

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Scan for Blueprint">
    First, ensure you have scanned a **hydroelectric turbine** to unlock its blueprint. These are usually found during exploration in various biomes.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Locate a Strong Current">
    Swim around until you find a natural current, identifiable by its **blue aura** and the push it gives your character. Use your builder tool to hover the turbine in the current and observe the power output number. Aim for the highest possible value.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Place the Turbine">
    Once you've found an optimal spot with a high power output reading, place the hydroelectric turbine. It will automatically orient itself with the current's flow.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Build a Power Relay System">
    From the turbine, you'll need to construct a series of **Power Transmitters** to carry the electricity back to your base. Place the first transmitter near the turbine, and then extend the line towards your base. Each transmitter will connect to the next, forming a power conduit.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Connect to Your Base">
    Ensure the final Power Transmitter in your relay system is placed close enough to connect directly to your base structure. This will integrate the turbine's generated power into your base's grid.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Optimizing Your Relay System">
To minimize the number of Power Transmitters needed and simplify your power grid, always try to build your base as close as possible to a strong natural current. This can allow you to connect the turbine directly or with only one or two transmitters.
</Callout>

</Section>
