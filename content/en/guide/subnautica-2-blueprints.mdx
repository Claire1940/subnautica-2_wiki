<Section icon={Zap} title="Advanced Resource Gathering">

Once you have successfully scanned the **subnautica 2 blueprints**, the real work begins: gathering the exotic materials required for the Modification Station. Unlike basic titanium or copper, the Feedback Resonator requires specialized components like Creature Enamel and Conduit.

Creature Enamel is found in large, bulbous deposits that look like grey placeholders. These are most abundant about 280 meters Southeast of the Alien Ruins. You will need to mine these nodes to produce Enameled Glass, a core component for high-pressure equipment.

<Section icon={Swords} title="The Crafting Process">

With the **subnautica 2 blueprints** in your PDA and the resources in your inventory, you must use specific crafting stations to assemble the final product. This isn't a simple fabricator job; it requires a multi-step refinement process involving the Configurator Processor and the Modification Station.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Refine Raw Materials">
    Use the Configurator Processor to create **Strongtanium** from your gathered ores. This material is required for the structural integrity of the resonator upgrade.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Fabricate Components">
    Head to your standard Fabricator to craft **Enameled Glass** (using Creature Enamel and Glass) and process your Conduit into usable circuit boards.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Access Modification Station">
    Open the "Prototype Tools" tab on your Modification Station. Select the Feedback Resonator upgrade. This will consume your basic Sonic Resonator and the new materials.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Field Testing">
    Locate a shielded Bloom Canker. Equip your new Feedback Resonator and hold the primary fire button. The shield should now shatter, allowing you to harvest the core.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Upgrade Complete">
The Feedback Resonator significantly increases your interaction range with organic structures. It is also more energy-efficient than the base model, making it a permanent slot-in for your hotbar during deep-sea expeditions.
</Callout>
