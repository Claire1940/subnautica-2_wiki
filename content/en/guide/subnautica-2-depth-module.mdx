<Section icon={Anchor} title="Understanding Depth Modules in Subnautica 2">
Depth modules are essential upgrades that increase the maximum crush depth of your submersible vehicles and even certain pieces of personal equipment. Without them, venturing beyond shallow waters will quickly lead to vehicle destruction and player death due to extreme pressure. In Subnautica 2, these modules are tied directly to your progression, allowing access to increasingly dangerous, yet rewarding, biomes.

<Callout type="tip">
Always check the maximum operating depth of your vehicle before diving into unknown territory. A simple glance can save you from a catastrophic crush.
</Callout>

The game emphasizes a discovery-first approach. This means you won't just be handed blueprints; you'll need to actively scan fragments and resources found in the environment to unlock the crafting recipes for these crucial modules. Co-op mechanics further enhance this, as shared scans mean one player's discovery can benefit the entire team.

### Crafting Process Overview

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Locate Blueprints">
    Explore new biomes and scan any wreckage, alien structures, or unique resource deposits you encounter. Fragment scans are crucial for unlocking new module recipes. In co-op, team members can share blueprint discoveries.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Gather Resources">
    Once a blueprint is unlocked, identify the required materials. These often include common metals, but higher-tier modules will demand rare ores and compounds found exclusively in deeper, more dangerous zones.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Use a Fabricator or Vehicle Upgrade Console">
    Most depth modules for your personal gear can be crafted at a standard fabricator. Vehicle depth modules, however, typically require a Vehicle Upgrade Console, often built within your base or directly into your submersible.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Install the Module">
    After crafting, open your vehicle's upgrade panel (usually accessed by interacting with the vehicle itself) and slot the newly crafted depth module into an available upgrade slot.
  </Step>
</Steps>
