<Section icon={Zap} title="Powering the Fully Functional Fabricator">

A fabricator is only as good as the power grid supporting it. In Subnautica 2, the **subnautica 2 fully functional fabricator** requires a constant draw of energy to process advanced materials. If your base runs out of power, the fabricator will cease to function, and your oxygen production will stop. Managing your "Watts" is a critical skill for any aspiring diver.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Solar Panel Placement">
    In the early game, Solar Panels are your best friend. Place them on the roof of your base. They generate maximum power when close to the surface and during daylight hours.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Connecting the Grid">
    Once placed, the Solar Panel automatically connects to any base structure within its radius. You will see the "Power" readout at the top of your screen increase.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Fabricator Installation">
    Use the Habitat Builder to place the Fabricator on an interior wall. Ensure the base has at least 10 units of power stored before attempting to craft.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Expansion">
    As you craft more items, consider adding a Bioreactor for 24/7 power, as Solar Panels do not work at night.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Success Check">
Once the fabricator is powered and placed in a base, interact with it. You will notice that the "corrupt" messages have disappeared, and all blueprints you have scanned are now fully available for crafting.
</Callout>

| Power Source | Energy Output | Pros | Cons |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Solar Panel | High (Day) | Renewable, Cheap | Zero night output |
| Bioreactor | Medium | Consistent, Uses waste | Requires constant refueling |
| Thermal Plant | Very High | Infinite energy | Requires heat vents |
