<Section icon={Swords} title="Aggressive Species Deep Dive">

The most immediate threats to your survival are the predatory leviathans. Unlike the solitary hunters of previous games, Subnautica 2 introduces pack mechanics that can overwhelm even the most experienced divers.

### Shiver Leviathan: The Void Stalker
Found exclusively in the Ecological Dead Zone (The Void), the Shiver Leviathan is a master of the deep. These creatures hunt in packs, usually consisting of one massive female and several smaller, more agile males. The Void itself reaches depths of 5,000 meters, providing these predators with a massive hunting ground.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Female Shiver">
    - **Massive Size**: The primary muscle of the pack.
    - **Lethal Force**: Capable of "one-tapping" players and destroying small vehicles instantly.
    - **Sonar Array**: Features long-range sensory organs to guide the pack.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Male Shiver">
    - **High Agility**: Smaller and faster than the female.
    - **Support Role**: Flanks the player while the female prepares a frontal assault.
    - **Sensory Dependence**: Relies on the female's sonar for navigation in the dark.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

### Collector Leviathan: The Tyrant Squid
The Collector Leviathan is a solitary, highly intelligent predator that resembles a massive octopus or squid. It is known for its "fearful curiosity," often investigating and "torturing" its prey before the final kill. It features three hearts and a hardened beak capable of tearing through titanium plating.

<Callout type="warning" title="Vehicle Warning">
The Collector Leviathan specifically targets large submarines. If you hear its thrusters engaging, immediately cut your engine and dim your lights to avoid detection.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="How to Scan the Collector Leviathan">

Scanning a Collector is one of the most difficult tasks in the game due to its speed and aggression. Follow these steps to secure the PDA data safely.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Prepare Distractions">
    Gather several **Tadpoles** or small indigenous fish. The Collector's curiosity makes it prone to investigating smaller movements, giving you a window to approach.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Locate a Chasm">
    Collectors are most at home in the **Outer Bounds**. Look for the "tar cascades" or deep chasms where they retreat to consume their prey.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Maintain Stealth">
    Approach using a specialized stealth biomod or by moving slowly along the trench walls. Avoid using lights, as the Collector's eyes are highly sensitive to artificial luminescence.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="The Scanning Window">
    Wait for the Collector to begin feeding on a **Great Jaw** or **Coral Crab**. This is the only time its aggression drops significantly.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Researcher Note">
Successfully scanning the Collector reveals that it shares biological similarities with Earth's squids, but with four specialized hunting tentacles tipped with bioglass claws.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Titan and Docile Giants">

Not every massive creature wants to eat you. Some **Subnautica 2 leviathans** serve as vital parts of the planet's history and atmosphere.

### The World Tree (Titan Class)
The World Tree is a massive, stationary organism that serves as a central plot point. While it cannot be scanned, its roots—known as **Titan Rock Bores**—are spread throughout the seabed. Rumors suggest an alien species may be in hibernation deep within its core, having failed to cure a planet-wide infection in the distant past.

### Deep Wing Bruder
The Deep Wing Bruder is a rare, filter-feeding leviathan that is completely docile. It is often found in the upper water columns, dropping blobs of oil as decoys to protect its actual, highly nutritious eggs.
