<Section icon={Target} title="Essential Scanning Techniques for Databank Entries">

Scanning is the cornerstone of data collection in Subnautica 2. Without it, you'll struggle to craft, build, or even understand the dangers lurking in the depths. Your PDA's scanner is your best friend. From small fish to massive leviathans, every scan adds valuable information.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Equip Your Scanner">
    Ensure your scanner is always equipped in your quick slot. This allows for quick access when encountering new flora, fauna, or wreckage. Crafting your first **Scanner** is a priority.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Prioritize Unknowns">
    When exploring, prioritize scanning any unknown objects or lifeforms. These will be highlighted, and scanning them often unlocks new Databank entries or blueprint fragments.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Wreckage and Habitats">
    Wreckages and abandoned habitats are goldmines for Databank entries. Look for data cards, terminals, and scannable fragments within these structures. For example, the **Old Habitat at Quaker signal location** contains numerous entries like Battery Terminal, Biobed, Bioreactor, Processor, Room, Scanner Station, and Spotlight.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Creature Scanning">
    Be cautious when scanning aggressive creatures. Often, a quick scan from a safe distance or using a vehicle can provide the necessary data without risking your life. Some creatures, like the **Needler Mango**, require specific strategies due to their aggressive nature.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Environmental Scans">
    Don't forget to scan environmental features like unique coral formations, geological nodes, and even anomalies. These can reveal resource locations or provide lore about Planet 4546B.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Depth and Oxygen Management">
Always monitor your oxygen and depth when exploring. Many valuable Databank entries are found in deeper or more complex cave systems. An **Air Bladder** and a reliable rebreather are essential for extended underwater excursions.
</Callout>

### Crafting the Tadpole Vehicle

The Tadpole is an invaluable asset for deeper exploration and data collection. Crafting it requires specific resources and access to a Vehicle Fabricator.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Unlock Tadpole Blueprints">
    Scan **Tadpole Fragments** found in various locations, such as 90m depth near the Welcome Center, or within the Colonist Bunker (Unauthorized NoA Modification). Scanning the second fragment unlocks the Moonpool blueprint.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Build a Moonpool">
    The Moonpool is a base module required for docking and building vehicles. It is unlocked after scanning a second Tadpole Fragment. Ensure you have sufficient titanium and quartz for its construction.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Construct Vehicle Fabricator">
    Inside your Moonpool, build the Vehicle Fabricator. This is where you will assemble your Tadpole. Resources needed include titanium ingots, copper ingots, and glass.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Gather Tadpole Materials">
    The Tadpole requires **Titanium Ingots**, **Copper Ingots**, and **Glass**. A critical component is the **Power Cell**, which needs strong acid. Strong acid can be made from sulfur and gold, or refined from specific blue crystalline nodes found northwest of your base.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Assemble and Upgrade">
    Once all materials are gathered, use the Vehicle Fabricator to assemble the Tadpole. Remember to research and craft upgrades like depth modules and storage modules to maximize its utility for data collection.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Utilizing Black Boxes and Colonist Logs

Black Boxes and Colonist Logs are narrative-rich Databank entries that provide context to the game's story and often point towards specific locations or objectives. They are typically found in wreckage sites or abandoned habitats. For instance, **Dr. Wu Lang-yi's black box** is located in a viral hot lab and his work may be your best lead.

### Recovering the Repair Tool

The **Repair Tool** is crucial for maintaining your vehicles and base components, especially after venturing into dangerous areas. It can be found in several locations:
- Inside **Chap's Blackbox wreckage** beneath a cave southeast of the lifepod.
- Within the **Colonist Bunker (Salvage)** in a cave around 200m north of the lifepod.
- Inside a wreck 250m north of the lifepod, which may require a **Sonic Resonator** to bypass Bloom Biofilm.
